Generation 1

Pokemon Red, Blue & Yellow

All the titles in this generation were released for the Game Boy. Pokemon Red & Blue were released in 1996 and Pokemon Yellow in 1998.

Learn more

Reviews

Generation 1 invented the Pokémon formula, providing a generation of childhoods with hours of fond memories of adventure, mystery and friendship. While there will always be an element of nostalgia involved when it comes to the first Pokémon generation, there's a lot to be said for Red and Blue's simple and intuitive systems, not to mention the iconic design of the original 150 Pokémon.

Generation 2

Pokemon Gold, Silver & Crystal

All the titles were released on the Game Boy Color. Pokemon Gold & Silver were released in 1999 and Pokemon Crystal in the year 2000.

Learn more

Reviews

Pokémon Gold, Silver, and Crystal set the benchmark for what can be achieved in a Pokémon adventure. Rather than abandoning the previous game as many of its sequels elected to do, Gold, Silver, and Crystal meaningfully built on Gen 1, attaching the Johto region to Kanto and allowing the player to adventure through both.

Generation 3

Pokemon Ruby, Sapphire and Emerald

All the titles in this generation were released for the Game Boy Advance. Pokemon Ruby & Sapphire were released in 2002 and Pokemon Emerald in 2004.

Learn more

Reviews

Generation 3 marked the first time that Pokémon released for a new console. The GameBoy Advance was a far more impressive machine than its predecessor, allowing for meaningful graphical improvements. Ruby and Sapphire were full of vibrant colors which gave the game a vivacity of which its predecessors could only dream.

Generation 4

Pokemon Diamond, Pearl and Platinum

All the titles in this generation were released for the Nintendo DS. Pokemon Diamond & Pearl were released in 2006 and Pokemon Platinum in 2008.

Learn more

Reviews

Pokémon Diamond, Pearl and Platinum were subtle but rewarding affairs, gently iterating on Ruby and Sapphire's improvements to the series without departing too far from what makes Pokémon great. Gen 4 also included some great designs. Piplup, Chimchar, and Turtwig rank among some of the most adorable starters in Pokémon history. The games also doubled down on the series' flirtations with mythology, adding Dialga, Palkia, and Arceus as new legendaries.

Generation 5

Pokemon Black & White, Black 2 & White 2

All the titles in this generation were released for the Nintendo DS. Pokemon Black & White were released in 2010 and Pokemon Black 2 & White 2 in 2012.

Learn more

Reviews

Pokémon Generation 5, particularly Black and White, featured a bold storytelling approach that questioned the game's core premise. Its sequel, Black and White 2, continued the narrative, reminiscent of the sequential worldbuilding seen in Pokémon Gold and Silver. The games also sparked controversy by allowing the reuse of TMs, a change that garnered mixed reactions from the fanbase.

Generation 6

Pokemon X & Y

All the titles in this generation were released for the Nintendo 3Ds. Pokemon X & Y were released in 2013.

Learn more

Reviews

Pokémon X and Y were a visual revolution for the Pokémon franchise, being the first games to be released on the 3DS console with truly 3D graphics. Graphics in X and Y were absolutely gorgeous, and we think we'll always remember our first-ever time in Lumiose City. Additionally, we got a new type (Fairy), Mega Evolution, Pokémon-Amie, and new character customization settings to go along with them.

Generation 7

Pokemon Sun & Moon, Pokemon Ultra Sun & Ultra Moon

All the titles in this generation were released for the Nintendo 3DS. Pokemon Sun & Moon were released in 2016 and Pokemon Ultra Sun & Ultra Moon in 2017.

Learn more

Reviews

No list of the best Pokémon games is complete without Sun and Moon, which introduced the Alola region. Alola offered a definite departure from the series' earlier environments, complete with region-specific Pokémon forms and challenges. Gone were gyms – replaced instead by island trials, and Totem Pokémon and themed challenges surrounding them. Even HMs disappeared. The novelty was certainly refreshing. Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon continued what Sun and Moon started, but rather than simply rehashing the same story in the kind of director's cut, they were more of an attempt to recreate the sequel structure we first saw with Black and White 2 in Generation 5.

Generation 8

Pokemon Sword & Shield

All the titles in this generation were released for the Nintendo Switch. Pokemon Sword & Shield were released in 2019..

Learn more

Reviews

Pokémon Sword and Shield were the first mainline Pokémon games to be released on the Nintendo Switch. Expectations were high from the initial announcement. But, despite the furor, the eighth generation seemed to weather the storm and both titles have gone on to have great critical and commercial success. Pokémon Sword and Shield’s Galar region, inspired by the United Kingdom, is charming and generally well-liked, and its wild areas added a much-requested open-world feature to the series.

Generation 9

Pokemon Scarlet & Violet

All the titles in this generation were released for the Nintendo Switch. Pokemon Scarlet & Violet are the newest games in the Pokemon franchise and were released in 2022.

Learn more

Reviews

Pokémon Scarlet and Violet was a double-edged sword, brimming with ambition in its attempt to rehabilitate Pokémon as an open-world game. The title enjoys three main storylines which players can complete at their own pace while exploring the expansive world of Paldea. However, the games feel half-finished, indicative, perhaps, of what Pokémon could be if it were given enough room to grow.
